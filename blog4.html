Noise pollution and its control

Suppose you are passing by a busy road and there is all traffic around you, the sound of horns buzzing your eardrums, in such a case what one is experiencing is noise pollution. Noise pollution is unwanted noise that may be harmful to the human ear and may cause adverse effects on the person's health. A sound level above 75db is harmful to the human ear, and longer exposure in a noisy area may have the same effects. If a person is standing in a traffic jam for more than 8 hours then certainly his eardrums are not facing a good phase of vibration.

A person in unusually a prolonged noise disturbance may face a lot of health issues like hypertension, cardiovascular problems, hearing problems, psychological dysfunction, sleep disturbances, etc. Noise pollution affects one's work performance.

Sound pollution has not only troubled human life but has stretched its arms to wildlife and marine life. One can notice on the beech that oceans are no quieter. Thousands of oil drills, sonars, seismic survey devices, coastal recreational watercraft, and shipping vessels are now populating our waters, and that is a serious cause of noise pollution for marine life.

A very important factor is that how we control noise pollution. A construction machine, traffic jam, a neighbour fond of hearing loud music in locality, can all bring about noise pollution. However there are some solutions to it too. For instance a person can wear earplugs for noise cancellation, less exposure to traffic areas, regular maintenance of vehicle and machines.
