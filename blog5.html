Technology is making us less human

We are surrounded by technology, for almost every work we do we make use of it. Look in your bedroom even a single day in the hot summers without the air conditioner might seem impossible to pass. But the question is if technology is making us fewer humans. I guess it is. Nowadays we don't need to meet people when we can just call them and social interaction is shifting from physical to virtual.

Technology has created a big wall of separation between humans beings. A person would rather sit in an empty room playing video games and texting on social media than meet and look for people around. We have google and alarms for reminders, and We are outsourcing the activities that require intelligence to technology. Thinking, remembering things, and analyzing are the essential qualities of humans.

Humans are social beings and they need interaction but technology has made our needs shift closer to itself making our feelings die away.

When someone is in trouble, some people are taking videos and pictures instead of helping them. This became a common thing these days. Dependence upon technology is affecting our morality, our system of values. This makes us wonder whether we are turning into machines?

Now the daily life of an average person passes on the smartphone or a computer and there is less time or say no urge for us to look after social bonds but to crave for technology. Hence, I think technology is making us less human
